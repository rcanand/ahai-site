Your notes are full of
"someday" and "maybe"

You're diligent about capturing ideas. Feature concepts, blog post drafts, research threads, things to explore when you have time. They go into your notes, tagged or linked, filed away with good intentions.

"I should try building that..." "Would be interesting to explore..." "TODO: come back to this"

But your note collection grows. Those forward-looking ideas get buried under meeting notes, project docs, and daily logs. The promising threads fade into the archive. Six months later, you rediscover them by accident—or you don't.

Surface what's been waiting

ahai uses local AI to read your markdown files and identify forward-looking concepts—ideas, intentions, and possibilities you captured but never acted on. It understands context, not just keywords. "Might be worth exploring" gets surfaced. Meeting minutes don't.

Point it at your Obsidian vault, a GitHub repo, or any folder of markdown. In minutes, you'll have a curated list of your own forgotten ideas, ready to review, organize, and finally do something with.

Built for how you work

Local AI Processing

Runs entirely on your Mac using MLX. Your notes are analyzed locally—nothing is sent to the cloud, ever.

Works with Your Tools

Compatible with Obsidian vaults, GitHub repos, or any folder of markdown files. No config needed.

Session Management

Pause and resume scans anytime. Pick up where you left off without re-processing your entire vault.

Drag-Drop Organization

Review surfaced ideas and organize them with simple drag-and-drop. Keep, dismiss, or categorize as you go.

Multiple Export Formats

Export your curated ideas to markdown, CSV, or JSON. Take them wherever your workflow goes.

Apple Silicon Optimized

Native performance on M1/M2/M3/M4 Macs. Fast inference without draining your battery.
